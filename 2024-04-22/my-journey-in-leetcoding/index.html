My Journey in LeetCoding

Posted on Mon 22 April 2024 Edited on Mon 29 April 2024 In Career Development , Interviews , Technical Coding Interviews Disqus: 691 3 mins.

After six months of consistent grinding on LeetCode and navigating through about twenty-ish coding interviews, I’ve finally landed a job at ByteDance. Far from a coding wizard, I’m your regular joe who just doesn’t give up on medium-level problems. I’ve pieced together a set of strategies that helped me punch above my weight, and who knows? They might just do the trick for you too!

Introduction to My LeetCode Strategy

Stepping into the coding challenges felt like entering a big arena. LeetCode became my training ground, and like many, I often stumbled on the tough problems. But the key? Stick it out and find your rhythm. Here’s how I kept my head in the game and punched through those coding challenges.

Personalised Practice Routine

Daily Goals

I made it my mantra to crack at least one or two problems daily. On turbo-charged days, I’d amp it up to three, juggling between easy, medium, and hard to stay sharp and resilient. Picture this: it’s like keeping your brain in a gym session, where every problem solved is a muscle flexed.

Timed Sessions

What’s a fight without a timer? I practised with the clock ticking, mirroring those nail-biting interview settings. (also LeetCode has a timer feature now!) Starting with 10 minutes for easy peasy ones, 30 for the tricky guys, and an hour for the brain-busters. As time went on, I got faster, nailing easies in 5 and hards in 30. You get this weird sixth sense for timing, and bro, does it make you feel like a coding ninja!

Review and Reflect

Every practice round was followed by a reflective huddle. What went well? What tripped me up? It’s like replaying game tapes after a match – always illuminating. Plus, post-interview, I’d rehash the questions, tackling similar ones to ensure the lesson sticks. My tip: Keep it mixed – a week on one topic or just random daily questions. Keeps the brain guessing and growing!

Weak Areas First

Diving headfirst into my fear zones, I prioritised my weak spots. It’s uncomfortable but hey, no growth in comfort zones, right?

Mock Coding Interview Sessions

Big shoutout to my friends for those mock sessions – totally priceless! Sure, there are platforms for this, but nothing beats real-time roasting and toasting from your pals. It’s all in good fun and massive gains, learning from each other’s quirks and queries.

Recommend the platform we used for practice: https://soc-tips.com/

Discipline Over Distraction

Here’s the kicker – I went on a digital detox. Social media, gaming, endless scrolling – chopped. My eyes were on the prize – landing a dream gig at a big tech company. Every spare moment? I was on LeetCode. Bus, gym, MRT – you name it, I was coding, even on my phone, drafting the diagram on my iPad… It was tough turning down hangouts and small job offers, but the focus was key.

This journey wasn’t just about nailing in coding; it was about setting a goal and going all out to achieve it. Hopefully, this sparks some ideas for you to build your own game plan, keeping it fun yet focused.

Favourite LeetCode Questions and Solutions

Stay tuned for my upcoming posts where I’ll dive into my favourite puzzles from my LeetCode escapades. Expect insights on everything from data structures to those mind-bending system design questions.

Conclusion: Keep Grinding, It Pays Off

Six months of intense coding and the results speak for themselves. Remember, perseverance is key. Just your average coder here, but one who stuck to the game plan and saw it through against the odds.

Looking forward to sharing more and tackling this coding journey with you. Let’s keep those codes compiling and our spirits high!

Encouragement and Final Thoughts

To all the tech aspirants out there in the trenches of coding challenges – keep pushing. Every line of code gets you closer to your goal. Excited to share more and learn together in this vibrant community. Let’s ace these challenges, one code at a time!
